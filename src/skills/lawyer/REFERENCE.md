# Lawyer - Reference Guide

## Contract Law Fundamentals

### Essential Elements of a Valid Contract

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Offer** | Clear proposal to enter agreement | "I'll sell you my car for $5,000" |
| **Acceptance** | Unequivocal agreement to terms | "I accept your offer" |
| **Consideration** | Something of value exchanged | Money, services, goods |
| **Capacity** | Legal ability to contract | Adults of sound mind |
| **Legality** | Purpose must be legal | Cannot contract for illegal acts |

### Contract Defenses (When Contracts May Be Invalid)

- **Duress**: Signed under threat or coercion
- **Fraud**: Intentional misrepresentation
- **Mistake**: Both parties mistaken about material fact
- **Unconscionability**: Extremely unfair terms
- **Lack of Capacity**: Minor, intoxicated, mentally incapacitated

## Intellectual Property

### Types of IP Protection

| Type | Protects | Duration | Registration |
|------|----------|----------|--------------|
| **Copyright** | Creative works (books, music, code) | Life + 70 years | Automatic (registration adds benefits) |
| **Trademark** | Brand names, logos, slogans | Indefinite (if maintained) | Recommended |
| **Patent** | Inventions, processes | 20 years (utility) | Required |
| **Trade Secret** | Confidential business info | Indefinite (if kept secret) | Not applicable |

## Employment Law Basics

### Protected Classes (Federal)

- Race, Color, National Origin
- Religion
- Sex (including pregnancy, gender identity)
- Age (40+)
- Disability
- Genetic Information

### Key Employment Laws

| Law | Coverage |
|-----|----------|
| Title VII | Discrimination (employers with 15+ employees) |
| ADA | Disability discrimination and accommodations |
| ADEA | Age discrimination (40+) |
| FLSA | Minimum wage, overtime, child labor |
| FMLA | Family and medical leave (50+ employees) |

## Landlord-Tenant Basics

### Typical Tenant Rights

- Habitable premises (working utilities, safety)
- Privacy (landlord notice before entry)
- Security deposit limits and return
- Protection from retaliation
- Proper eviction procedures

### Typical Landlord Rights

- Receive rent on time
- Property maintained in reasonable condition
- Entry with proper notice (emergency exceptions)
- Evict for valid causes through legal process

*Note: Laws vary significantly by state/locality*

## Civil vs Criminal Law

| Aspect | Civil Law | Criminal Law |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Parties** | Private parties | State vs. accused |
| **Purpose** | Compensation/resolution | Punishment/deterrence |
| **Burden of Proof** | Preponderance of evidence | Beyond reasonable doubt |
| **Outcome** | Damages, injunctions | Fines, imprisonment |
| **Examples** | Contracts, torts, property | Theft, assault, fraud |

## Court System Overview

### Federal Courts

1. **District Courts**: Trial level
2. **Circuit Courts of Appeals**: Appellate level
3. **Supreme Court**: Highest court

### State Courts

1. **Trial Courts**: Various names (Superior, Circuit, District)
2. **Appellate Courts**: Review trial decisions
3. **Supreme Court**: Highest state court

### Jurisdiction

- **Subject Matter**: What type of cases court can hear
- **Personal**: Court's power over parties
- **Federal Question**: Cases involving federal law
- **Diversity**: Cases between citizens of different states (>$75,000)

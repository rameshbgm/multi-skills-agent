# Financial Analyst - Reference Guide

## Key Financial Concepts

### Valuation Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Interpretation |
|--------|---------|----------------|
| P/E Ratio | Price / Earnings Per Share | Lower = potentially undervalued |
| P/B Ratio | Price / Book Value | Compares market to asset value |
| EV/EBITDA | Enterprise Value / EBITDA | Used for comparing companies |
| PEG Ratio | P/E / Earnings Growth Rate | Accounts for growth |

### Profitability Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Good Range |
|--------|---------|------------|
| ROE | Net Income / Shareholders' Equity | 15-20%+ |
| ROA | Net Income / Total Assets | 5%+ |
| Gross Margin | (Revenue - COGS) / Revenue | Industry dependent |
| Net Margin | Net Income / Revenue | 10%+ for most |

### Liquidity Ratios

| Metric | Formula | Healthy Range |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | 1.5 - 3.0 |
| Quick Ratio | (Current Assets - Inventory) / Current Liabilities | > 1.0 |

## Investment Vehicles

### Stocks

- **Growth Stocks**: High earnings growth, reinvest profits
- **Value Stocks**: Undervalued relative to fundamentals
- **Dividend Stocks**: Regular income through dividends
- **Blue Chips**: Large, established, stable companies

### Bonds

- **Government Bonds**: Treasury, municipal (lower risk)
- **Corporate Bonds**: Investment grade vs high-yield
- **Bond Ratings**: AAA to D (credit quality)

### Funds

- **Mutual Funds**: Actively managed, higher fees
- **Index Funds**: Track market indexes, lower fees
- **ETFs**: Trade like stocks, tax efficient

## Retirement Planning

### Account Types

| Account | Tax Treatment | Contribution Limit (2024) |
|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| 401(k) | Pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal | $23,000 |
| Roth IRA | After-tax contributions, tax-free growth | $7,000 |
| Traditional IRA | Pre-tax contributions, taxed on withdrawal | $7,000 |

### The 4% Rule

- Withdraw 4% of portfolio annually in retirement
- Historically sustainable for 30-year retirement
- Adjust based on market conditions and personal needs

## Risk Metrics

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Beta | Volatility relative to market (1.0 = market) |
| Alpha | Excess return above benchmark |
| Sharpe Ratio | Risk-adjusted return (higher = better) |
| Standard Deviation | Measure of price volatility |
